## 2. LINKEDIN (Professional)

I built an LMS from scratch. Here's what it does.

GrassLMS is a learning management system designed for schools teaching programming, mathematics, and languages.

The problem: Most LMS platforms are either too simple (just video + quiz) or too complex and expensive for small schools. None of them make coding or math genuinely interactive.

What makes GrassLMS different:

Code execution in 37 languages with auto-graded challenges. Students write real code, run it against test cases, and get instant feedback. Python, JavaScript, Java, C++, Go, Rust -- all in the browser.

Interactive math with 15+ exercise templates. Not just multiple choice -- students drag points on coordinate planes, balance equations on visual scales, graph functions, manipulate fractions visually.

Game-based learning. 2D robot programming puzzles where students write commands to navigate grids. 3D worlds where they explore, collect items, and solve spatial puzzles.

AI tutor that uses the Socratic method -- guides students through problems without giving away answers. Context-aware: it knows what lesson and exercise the student is working on.

Full SAT Math prep module with timed practice tests, domain-based question banks, and integrated Desmos calculator.

For teachers: course builder with 9 content types and 11 exercise formats, gradebook, analytics dashboard, assignment management, review queue, and a reusable content library.

Gamification that keeps students engaged: XP, streaks, badges, a 5-tier league system, and class leaderboards.

Plus: learning paths, certificates, calendar, parent portal, discussions, dark mode, PWA support, and localization in 4 languages.

Tech stack: Next.js, FastAPI, PostgreSQL, Docker. Self-hosted on a single VPS for under $5/month.

Now onboarding the first student groups.
