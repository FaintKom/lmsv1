The discriminant formula

When you can't factor cleanly, the discriminant tells you everything about the roots — how many there are and what kind. It's the cheat sheet you'll actually use.

The setup

Take any quadratic in standard form: ax² + bx + c = 0. The discriminant is the part of the quadratic formula that lives under the square root.

Δ = b² − 4ac

That single number — capital delta, Δ — decides everything. Three cases, no exceptions:

!
Why this matters

Before you spend 5 minutes solving, plug in a, b, c and compute Δ. If it's negative, the equation has no real solutions — you'll save yourself time. If it's zero, there's exactly one. If it's positive, two. That's it.

Quick check

Let's try one before moving on. Take a moment, work it out on paper if you need to.

Q1
Multiple choice · 10 XP

For 2x² − 4x + 5 = 0, what is the discriminant?

Plug a=2, b=−4, c=5 into Δ = b² − 4ac and choose the right answer.

A Δ = 16
B Δ = −24 ✓
C Δ = 24
D Δ = 4
Need a hint?

What does the answer tell us?

Δ = −24 is negative — so this equation has no real solutions. The parabola never crosses the x-axis. We'll see what that looks like graphically in the next lesson.
