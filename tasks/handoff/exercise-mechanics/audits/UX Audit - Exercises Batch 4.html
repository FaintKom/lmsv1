UX audit — cards & tables, batch 4

Seven mechanics reviewed: card sort, SRS flashcards, Venn (numbers), Venn (items), two-way table, table pattern, area model. Two findings in this batch are showstoppers: a whole input modality (touch) silently broken in two mechanics, and one mechanic that prints its own answer on screen.

Sources: components/exercises/v2/{card-sort,srs-flashcard,venn-diagram,venn-elements,two-way-table,table-pattern,area-model}-v2.tsx  →  open the playground · audits: 1 · 2 · 3

Showstopper №1 — HTML5 drag-and-drop on touch devices. CardSortV2 and VennElementsV2 use draggable / onDragStart / onDrop. Mobile Safari and Chrome on Android do not fire these events for touch input — a child on a tablet or phone cannot move a single card. The rest of the family (categorize, ordering, matching) already uses pointer events; these two predate that pattern. Ported to pointer events + tap-to-arm in the playground.
Showstopper №2 — TablePattern prints the answer. The rule input renders placeholder={ruleDisplay} — the literal correct rule, faintly, inside the box the child must fill. Replaced with a neutral example placeholder ("like: 3x − 2").

8 · Batch-4 cross-cutting

The retry contract is now uniform across the whole family. After 4 batches the rule is worth writing down for production: (1) correct parts lock green and cannot be broken; (2) wrong parts either hop back (spatial mechanics) or clear their mark on edit (typed mechanics); (3) the sheet leads with the win ("3 of 5 right"); (4) the answer never appears while retries remain; (5) at 0 hearts the reveal is a structured list with human labels. Implementing this once in a shared useRetryContract() hook would collapse ~300 lines of duplicated logic across 24 components.
