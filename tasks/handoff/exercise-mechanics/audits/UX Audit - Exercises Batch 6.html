Design principles that emerged (write these into the design system)

  1. Meaning first, form coached. Case, punctuation, accents, single typos and equivalent numeric forms never cost a heart — they earn a gentle note.
  2. Three tones, always: green = you did it · coral = a miss · grey = the system hiccuped (never the child's fault, never costs anything).
  3. The answer is sacred: it never appears — in any channel, including placeholders and ARIA — until the task is truly over.
  4. Retries protect progress: correct parts lock green and become untouchable; the sheet always leads with what went right.
  5. Every drag has a tap twin: arm-and-place is simultaneously the touch fallback, the keyboard path, and the screen-reader path.
  6. Check is earned, never free: it unlocks on interaction, and a blocked press wiggles + explains instead of dead-clicking.
  7. Feedback names the dial, not the direction: "the slope is off" guides; "go higher" solves it for them.
  8. Playfulness is a dial: every animation runs through --mamp/--mdur — calm for assessments, bouncy for the youngest, off for reduced-motion.
