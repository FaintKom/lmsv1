UX audit — graphs & math surfaces, batch 5

Eight mechanics reviewed: coordinate plane, function graph, graph transform, inequality graph, scatter plot, function machine, probability wheel, equation balance. The family theme: gorgeous interactive surfaces that assume a 380px-wide mouse-driven world. Every fix here is about making them survive fingers, keyboards, and 320px panes.

Sources: components/exercises/v2/{coordinate-plane,function-graph,graph-transform,inequality-graph,scatter-plot,function-machine,probability-wheel,equation-balance}-v2.tsx + _grid-axes.tsx  →  open the playground · audits: 1 · 2 · 3 · 4

Showstopper — scatter plot is the third touch-broken mechanic. Drag handles listen to onMouseDown/onMouseMove only — no pointer events, no touch path at all. Combined with batch 4's findings (card sort, venn elements), that's three mechanics where a tablet child simply cannot answer. Ported to pointer events with capture + keyboard arrows.

4 · Probability wheel probability-wheel-v2.tsx

IDTypeFinding → recommendation
PW-01UX20 mandatory spins × 1.5s = a 30-second choreThe first spins are exciting; by spin 9 it's labour, and the lesson stalls. After 3 single spins a "×5 quick" button appears: one short animation, five tallies. The child keeps the feel of spinning but reaches the data in ~15s. (Default scenario also tuned to 12 spins.) SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
PW-02UXLocked picker looks brokenTally rows are disabled until all spins are done, with no explanation — children tap them and nothing happens. A status line now narrates the phase: "Spin first — then you'll pick the winner here" → "Pick the slice that came up most ↑" → "Now hit Check!". SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
PW-03UXSuccess misses the teachable momentWhen the empirical winner ISN'T the biggest slice, that's the whole point of probability — the sheet now celebrates it: "Fun twist: the biggest slice is Red — luck wobbles in small experiments!" SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND

5 · Equation balance equation-balance-v2.tsx

IDTypeFinding → recommendation
EB-01BUGHardcoded "−4" buttonThe op row ships literal subtractWeight(1) and subtractWeight(4) buttons — on any equation whose weights aren't 4, the second button is permanently dead. Ops are now generated from the scale state: −1, −minW (the biggest balanced subtraction available), −x when both sides have x, ÷n when it splits evenly. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
EB-03UXNo undoOne mis-tap (say −x too early) and the only way out is failing the check to reset. Undo button + move history chips. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
EB-04UXDisabled ÷ is a mystery÷ greys out when weights don't divide evenly, silently. A caption explains: "÷ is sleeping: the weights don't split evenly yet". SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
EB-05UXx-on-both-sides configs unreachable in upstream opsWith ops fixed (EB-01), "3x + 1 = x + 7" works — added as a scenario since it's the pedagogically interesting case. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND

7 · Batch-5 cross-cutting

SVG interaction checklist for production (every graph mechanic, current and future): ① viewBox + width:100%, never fixed px; ② pointer events with setPointerCapture, never mouse events; ③ touch-action:none on the interactive surface only; ④ every draggable is focusable with arrow-key movement; ⑤ pointer→data math goes through the rendered rect ratio, not viewBox units; ⑥ hit targets ≥ 28px SVG units (scales to ≥44px at typical render sizes).
