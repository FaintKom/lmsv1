UX audit — math mechanics, batch 2

Five math mechanics reviewed: numeric input, MC math, number line, equation solver, math stepwise. Same lens as batch 1 — kid clarity (7–14), feedback richness, adaptiveness — plus a math-specific one: can a child physically enter every answer the methodist can author? Twice the answer was no.

Sources: components/exercises/v2/{numeric-input,mc-math,number-line,equation-solver,math-stepwise}-v2.tsx  →  open the playground · batch 1 audit

1 · Numeric input numeric-input-v2.tsx

Strong concept: formula card + on-screen pad + collapsible worked example. The pad is the primary input for kids — and it's where the problems are.

IDTypeFinding → recommendation
NI-02BUGNegative answers cannot be entered on the padThe pad has digits, "." and ⌫ — no minus. Any exercise with a negative answer (temperature, debts, coordinates) is unsolvable without a hardware keyboard. Added a ± key that toggles the sign. Try the "Negative answer" scenario. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
NI-03BUGComma decimals count as wrongRussian/European keyboards (and habits) produce "2,5"; parseFloat reads it as 2 → marked wrong, heart lost, child confused. Sanitise input: comma → dot, one dot max, digits only. Also blocks "3..14" and "1.2.3" which the field happily accepted. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
NI-01ADAPTPad keys are ~37px tallpadding: 8px 0 on the primary touch surface for 7-year-olds — under the 44px minimum the project's own UX guide mandates. Keys now ≥48px with the signature press physics (shadow-pop), and a proper disabled state (they previously looked live during feedback). SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
NI-04A11YUnlabeled input and pad keysThe answer field has no accessible name; "⌫" reads as "backslash" or nothing. aria-label everywhere; delete is now a labelled full-width key. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
NI-06UXThe worked example hides exactly when it's neededA child who just lost a heart doesn't think to open the collapsed "Example" panel. After a wrong attempt the hint card pulses and the sheet nudges toward it ("Psst — the example might help"). SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND

3 · Number line number-line-v2.tsx

The marker physics, value bubble and tick magnet are excellent feedback grammar. The gaps: a free win, a lockout, and label collisions.

IDTypeFinding → recommendation
NL-03BUGThe marker can start on the answerStart position is the range midpoint; whenever the target IS the midpoint (range 0–10, answer 5 — a very common authoring pattern) the child presses Check and wins without touching anything. Start now offsets by one step when it would coincide. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
NL-04UXCheck enabled before any interactioncanCheck={true} always — combined with NL-03 this rewards doing nothing, and generally invites a no-thought first tap. Check unlocks after the marker moves; pressing early gives the standard nudge + "Move the marker first". SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
NL-01A11YPointer-only — no keyboard pathThe marker is now a real slider: role="slider" with value semantics, ←/→ moves by step, Home/End jumps to the ends, value bubble flashes on each move. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
NL-02ADAPTEvery tick gets a label — they collideRange −10…10 step 1 = 21 labels; at 320px that's ~13px per label. Labels now thin automatically (≈44px budget per label, measured via ResizeObserver), keeping zero, the ends, and every k-th tick. Try "Dense ticks" at 320. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
NL-05UXWrong-attempt feedback says nothing useful"You placed 6" — the child knows. Tell them something actionable without giving it away: direction ("try further right"). Distance stays secret so it's still a thought, not a binary search. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND

4 · Equation solver equation-solver-v2.tsx

IDTypeFinding → recommendation
ES-01UXWrong picks are free — brute force is optimalNo hearts: a wrong option shakes and… nothing. With 3 options per step, tapping everything is faster than thinking, and the comment admits it ("methodist can wrap a retry budget at a higher layer" — none does). Hearts wired in: a wrong move costs one and crosses the option out; at zero the full solution chain is revealed step by step. Consistent with the rest of the family. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
ES-04UXNo sense of progress through the solveMulti-step task with no progress indicator. The shell's step bar now shows "2 / 3"; new chain rows pop in. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
ES-02UXPrivate shake keyframesThe component ships its own <style>@keyframes gp-shake — a near-duplicate of the system's fb-shake, ignoring --mamp/--mdur (so the playfulness dial and reduced-motion clamp don't reach it). Use the shared one. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
ES-03UXDouble-tap double-counts a wrong optionNothing disables an option during/after its shake — two quick taps = two wrong attempts. Wrong options now eliminate on first tap. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND

5 · Math stepwise math-stepwise-v2.tsx

IDTypeFinding → recommendation
MS-02BUG"x = 5.0" is wrong, "x=5" is rightMatching is literal after stripping whitespace/case. Equivalent numeric forms fail: 5.0 ≠ 5, .5 ≠ 0.5, 16 = 4x ≠ 4x = 16. For children this is the cruellest kind of wrong — they solved the math. Added numeric-equivalence + flipped-sides comparison; full CAS-grade checking stays out of scope. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
MS-01UXCorrect lines stay editable on retryThe retry flow preserves values and focuses the first bad line (nice!) — but a child can accidentally mangle an already-correct line. Correct lines now lock with a green tint, and the sheet leads with the win: "1 of 2 lines are right". SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
MS-05UXReveal is a run-on string"Step 1: 4x = 16 · Step 2: x = 4" in one line — structured list, same as MT-03/CT-04. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
MS-03A11YStep labels aren't labels"Step 1" is a span; the input has no accessible name. Now a real <label for>. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND

6 · Batch-2 cross-cutting

Pattern worth a shared helper: answer reveal discipline. Three components (TrueFalse, McMath, and partially NumberLine's ghost) hand-roll "when do we show the correct answer" and two of three got it wrong. A single revealAllowed(feedback, attemptsLeft) in the family — or a shell-level prop — closes the class of bugs.
IDTypeFinding → recommendation
XM-01UXMath entry ≠ math authoringAudit rule for every math mechanic going forward: for each answer the config can express, verify the child can physically produce it with the provided input surface (pad keys, snap step, option set). NI-02 and NL-03 both fail this test today. PROPOSED as an authoring-time validation in the exercise config panel
XM-02ADAPTFormula cards don't scale.fb-formula was fixed 24px / 26px padding; now clamp()-scaled with container width, matching the batch-1 container-query approach. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
XM-03UXKaTeX dependency unverified hereProduction uses MaybeMath for LaTeX. The playground renders plain text/Unicode math; once we port batch 5 (graphs), it's worth loading KaTeX in the lab to verify math layout at 320px too. PROPOSED
