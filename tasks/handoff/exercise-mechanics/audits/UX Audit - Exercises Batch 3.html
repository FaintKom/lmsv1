UX audit — language mechanics, batch 3

Six language mechanics reviewed: sentence builder, translation, conjugation, dialogue, reading, crossword. The batch-specific lens: language answers are fuzzy by nature — children type, and typing means caps, missing accents, stray spaces and full stops. Grading that punishes form over meaning is the #1 source of unfair hearts in this family.

Sources: components/exercises/v2/{sentence-builder,conjugation,translation,dialogue,reading,crossword}-v2.tsx  →  open the playground · batch 2 · batch 1

6 · Crossword crossword-v2.tsx

The most keyboard-centric mechanic in the family — and upstream had almost no keyboard behaviour beyond typing into a focused cell.

IDTypeFinding → recommendation
CW-01UXNo auto-advanceTyping a letter left the caret in place; children had to tap every cell. Typing now walks along the active word (skipping locked cells); Backspace clears and walks back. This single fix roughly halves the interaction cost of the whole mechanic. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
CW-02UXCrossing cells are ambiguousA cell in both an across and a down word had no way to choose which word you're typing. Tapping the focused crossing cell toggles direction; the active word's clue highlights, and tapping any clue jumps to its first cell. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
CW-03UXRetry wipes nothing — and protects nothingCorrect letters now lock green on retry ("14 of 17 letters are right"), wrong ones stay editable. SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND
CW-05UXFailure reveal was all-coral noiseAt 0 hearts the missing letters now fill in as dashed-green "ghost" letters — visually "here's what you were missing", not "everything is wrong". SHIPPED IN PLAYGROUND

7 · Batch-3 cross-cutting

Grading philosophy for typed language: meaning first, form coached. Three tiers, used consistently across translation/conjugation/stepwise: (1) exact → celebrate; (2) equivalent-but-imperfect (case, punctuation, accents, 1 typo) → accept or free retry, with a gentle note about the imperfection; (3) wrong → heart. The cruel middle tier is where children unfairly lose hearts today.

XL-02UXCyrillic support is untested in gradingNormalisation (normLang, accent stripping) handles Latin diacritics; Russian content (ё ≡ е is a real equivalence kids expect) needs a locale-aware pass before batch rollout. PROPOSED
XL-03UXTTS exists nowhere in the language familyFor 7–10-year-olds, hearing the sentence is half the exercise. The Web Speech API is free and offline-capable; a speaker button on translation sources and dialogue bubbles is a cheap, high-value add (styled .fb-speak is already in the playground CSS). PROPOSED
